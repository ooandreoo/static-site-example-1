Goalkeeping 101

Basics

Within a football team there is one single player whose actions define the result of the game, because strikers can miss lots of possible goals, score at the very last minute and they would have done their job. However, a goalkeeper has to give its maximum effort not to miss a single ball, because a miss means a goal for the other team (unless the oponent striker misses).

There is no need to get anxious though, nobody is expecting top class performances unless you are at first division league (and even there you can see some bloopers once in a while). It is important to have this in mind because as a not professional goalkeeper you need to build confidence first of all, because almost every action a goalkeeper executes requires a good amount of confidence in your own capabilities and skills.

As a goalkeeping fan and having been a goalkeeper myself during my childhood and part of my youth, I identified later in my life which are some of the basic skills and capabilites that everyone trying to become a better goalkeeper should give some training time to. The physical capabilities are:

  • Flexibility
  • Good reflexes
  • Physical Strength
  • Explossiveness
The soft skills to consider are:
  • Self confidence
  • Be able to communicate with defenders
  • Resillience
  • It is just a game mentality
The goalkeeping skills you need to focus are:
  • Scoop
  • Cup
  • W
  • Diving W

Equipment

The basic equipment you need is a pair of gloves, doesn't matter if they are not fancy to start. You only need a little bit of the grappling advantage of the glove and it makes it a bit easier to punch the ball.

If you are training on sintetic field, you probably would wanna wear long sleeve shirts and goalkeeper pants, or long socks, because your falling technique won't be as polished yet at the beginning, so be prepared to get a bit hurt.

Training Tips

Goalkeeper training should be different than the average player in some specific ways, like more explosiveness in the legs because usually goalkeepers need to be really fast within their area to shorten the distance between them and the ball before the attackers strike.

Following with the legs, another thing to consider training is the jump. To the side, to the top, low range, wide range, you better train this variations of jumps because they will be part of your routine as a goalkeeper while at the game. I would emphasize to train this specific type of jump:

  1. another team player send a high ball to the area (from the corner or any other place on the field)
  2. another team players act as attackers kind of difficulting your mobility and or visibility in the area (you can use sticks or something similar instead of people for the obstacles)
  3. train your mobility while watching the ball and match it with a good jump to catch or punch the ball while it is still high enough so that no attacker can hit it with their heads
That specific exercise is a true gem of a train, because many goalkeepers focus on doing great saves from long shoots directly to the goal, but few know how to intercept a high ball going into their area.

Other type of explosiveness that goalkeepers need to train is the one involving arms and core. These ones are important because different to the rest of the players, goalkeepers sometimes end up laying on the floor and they need to recover from that position to be ready to receive another strike or to start a counter attack. So pushups and working out your core is a must as part of your training as a goalkeeper.

Finally, always train with the team players that will act as barrier on the free kicks. Always talk to one of them to help you set the barrier, so that this player moves the barrier to the left or the right according to your preference. You need to stick to the side of the goal and the barrier should obstruct a direct shot from the ball position, so move it until you can no longer see the ball, and then go to the other side of the goal and wait.
